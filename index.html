Top Attractions & Landmarks

Rocky Mountain National Park

Established in 1915, this iconic park offers 415 square miles of pristine wilderness, including the famous Bear Lake Trail and challenging Longs Peak summit.

Rich Mountain Heritage

~10,000 Years Ago

Native American Heritage

Ute and Arapaho tribes summered in the Estes Park area, drawn by the abundant wildlife and natural beauty of the high country.

1800s

Mountain Men & Explorers

Adventurous explorers and "mountain men" arrived seeking beaver pelts and bear skins. Major Stephen H. Long led one of the first organized explorations in 1820.

1903

F.O. Stanley's Arrival

Steam-powered car inventor Freelan Oscar Stanley came to Estes Park seeking curative mountain air for tuberculosis. His health improved dramatically over the summer.

1909

The Stanley Hotel Opens

F.O. Stanley opened his grand hotel on July 4th as a resort for upper-class Easterners and health retreat, helping put Estes Park on the map.

1915

Rocky Mountain National Park

Rocky Mountain National Park was established, with Stanley's support and advocacy playing a crucial role in its creation.

1917

Estes Park Incorporated

The town of Estes Park was officially incorporated, establishing it as Colorado's gateway to the Rockies.
